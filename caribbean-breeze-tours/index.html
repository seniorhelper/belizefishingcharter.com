Fishing Ambergris Caye · Belize

The waters off San Pedro are some of the most productive in the Caribbean

Ambergris Caye sits right on the edge of the Belize Barrier Reef — the second-largest reef system on the planet and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. That single fact is why a San Pedro fishing charter can put you on three completely different fisheries in the same morning. Turtle-grass flats hold bonefish, permit and tarpon a few hundred yards off the beach. The reef itself is stacked with snapper, grouper and jacks. And just past the drop-off, the deep blue opens up for mahi-mahi, kingfish, wahoo and tuna.

Caribbean Breeze Tours has been fishing this stretch of water for more than fifteen years. Seasons & species

When to come for what you want to catch

San Pedro fishes well all year, but the target species shift with the seasons. Here's a quick guide so you can time your trip — or just know what's realistic when you're already here.

Flats species (bonefish, permit, tarpon) are the constant. Bonefish and permit are on the flats essentially year-round, which is why serious light-tackle and fly anglers put Ambergris Caye on their bucket list. Migratory tarpon are strongest from roughly March through September, when the big fish move through the channels.

Deep sea pelagics follow the water temperature. Mahi-mahi (dorado) peak in the cooler, calmer months of December through April — the same window that also brings the flat, glassy seas that make offshore trips comfortable. Kingfish and wahoo run strong from November into May, and blackfin tuna show up in the warmer stretch from April through August.

Reef fishing never really switches off. Snapper, grouper, jacks and barracuda are catchable all twelve months, which makes reef trips the safe, high-action choice no matter when you land in Belize. If your dates are locked and you just want to bend a rod and eat well, the reef delivers every time.

One practical note on weather: Belize's dry season (roughly late November to May) generally means calmer seas and easier offshore days, while the green season can bring afternoon showers. Caribbean Breeze Tours fishes year-round and simply adjusts — a windy day offshore becomes a protected flats or reef day. Tell them your dates and they'll tell you honestly what's fishing best.

Planning your day

How to book & what to bring

Getting there. Charters launch from Texaco Marina on Ambergris Caye, right in San Pedro Town. If you're staying on the island you're minutes away by golf cart or taxi. Flying in? San Pedro has its own airstrip with quick puddle-jumper hops from Belize City's international airport (about 15 minutes in the air), or you can take the water taxi if you prefer the scenic route.

Booking. The simplest way is to book directly through their website or give them a call — trips in the busy December-through-April window fill up, so a few days' notice is smart. Tell them how many are in your party, whether you're chasing flats, reef or deep sea, and whether anyone's new to fishing. They'll match you to the right boat and captain.

What's included. Rods, reels, tackle and bait are provided, along with the captain and mate. Most trips include water and sometimes light snacks — confirm when you book. For deep sea trips they'll have the heavier trolling gear ready.

What to bring. Reef-safe sunscreen (Belize protects its reef, so leave the harsh stuff at home), a hat, polarized sunglasses, a light long-sleeve for sun, and a dry bag for your phone. Bring a small cooler if you plan to take fish back to your rental. Cash for the crew tip is always appreciated. Seasick-prone? Take something before an offshore day — the flats and reef are calmer if that's a concern.
